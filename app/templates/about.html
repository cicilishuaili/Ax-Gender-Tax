About this tool

This tool was partly inspired by a report by New York Cityâ€™s Department of Consumer Affairs from 2015, called From Cradle to Cane: The Cost of Being a Female Consumer

The report revealed the existence of a "pink tax", in which the average woman pays 7% more for everyday products and services, translating to an extra $1,351 a year. Women paid more 42% of the time while men paid more 18% of the time.

This finding was corroborated in the analysis performed in building this tool, as deodorants for women were found to cost, on average, about 8% more than deodorants for men.

The goal of the tool is to help both men and women see any potential gender tax imposed and raise awareness so as to have more of a choice in the product attributes they are willing to pay more for.

Some of them are brands that have aligned to a specific gender (Secret vs. Old Spice), some are application related (floral, moisturizer), while others are somewhat subjective applications of adjectives (beautiful, incredible vs. endurance, energized).
